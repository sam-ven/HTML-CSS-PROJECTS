      While the exact date of the Markandeya Temple's establishment is unknown, it is clear that the temple holds centuries of history. Its tourism history can be traced back to when pilgrimages to holy sites became an integral part of Hindu culture. As part of the Kshetra Yatra, or sacred pilgrimage, the temple has seen a steady flow of visitors seeking blessings and offering prayers. In the past few decades, the temple has seen significant improvements and renovations, which have both preserved and enhanced its historic charm and spiritual ambiance. These changes have made the temple more accessible and attractive to tourists, further deepening its tourism history.

   The latest trends in tourism at the Markandeya Temple reflect a blend of spirituality and modernity. Today, visitors not only come for religious purposes but also to appreciate the temple's architecture, participate in cultural festivals, and enjoy the serene environment. The temple authorities have embraced digital technology, offering online services such as virtual darshans and live-streaming of events, catering to the needs of devotees who cannot visit in person. Eco-friendly initiatives are also part of the current tourism trend. Efforts have been made to keep the temple premises and the adjoining areas clean and green.
